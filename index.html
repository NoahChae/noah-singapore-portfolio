My Singapore Experience

What did you learn about Singaporean culture?

I learned that the Singapore culture is a melting pot of numerous other cultures including Malay, Chinese, Indian, Thai, and more. Singaporeans are often more collective and unified when it comes to office culture. In my experience, the workspace was open, intentionally arranged to encourage communication with peers, supervisors, and even the CEO.

What did you find challenging about studying and working abroad?

Assimilating to the “high-context” culture of Singapore was definitely a challenge when it came to communication in the office. An example of this occurred at my company when I was given initial instructions and there was a misalignment of expectations. I was given a project to redesign and restructure their main website, however was not given clear design guidelines. My initial assumptions did not align with the company’s vision and thus I learned to infer with intent from that moment onwards, calibrating to high-context cues.

What were some lessons you learned through your experience?

Professionally, I learned valuable skills pertaining to working in a corporate environment where I had to develop soft skills such as being persistent with asking questions, empathizing with users, and being adaptable in uncomfortable circumstances. I also was able to learn the whole design process through the two main projects I had the opportunity to work on, as well as learn the importance of understanding the purpose of design in any situation.

How will you use your intercultural experience when you return to UF?

At UF, I will bring a global lens when it comes to communication, understanding that many people have different styles of communication. I must be willing to adapt and allow myself to be teachable, both things that I believe I was able to grow during my time in Singapore.

Tools

Objective

The primary goal of this project was to create a platform to showcase the MetaVerse product. I aimed to attract clients by clearly communicating the platform’s value proposition and drive conversions through two main calls-to-action: “Contact Us” (to initiate sales inquiries) and “Get Started” (to view pricing plans or begin a trial).

Wireframe

This wireframe is a low-fidelity visual layout that outlines the structure of the Metaverse website; the wireframe serves as a blueprint that focuses on functionality, content placement, and user flow, rather than detailed visuals or branding.

Outcome

Blue and purple were chosen as the primary colors for the design system to showcase a modern and tech feel. The platform enables corporate clients to create custom virtual environments to showcase and sell their products.
